Science Behind It
How Drug–Food
Interactions Work

Food compounds can alter how your body absorbs, metabolizes, or excretes medications — sometimes dangerously increasing or decreasing drug levels in your blood.

01
Enzyme Inhibition
Food compounds like furanocoumarins in grapefruit block CYP3A4 liver enzymes, causing drugs to accumulate to dangerous levels.
02
Absorption Interference
Dairy calcium, iron in spinach, or high-fiber foods can bind to drug molecules in the gut, reducing absorption into the bloodstream.
03
Vitamin K Antagonism
Blood thinners like Warfarin work by blocking Vitamin K. Eating large amounts of leafy greens floods the system and reduces drug effect.
04
Tyramine Crisis
MAO inhibitors prevent tyramine breakdown. Eating aged cheese or cured meats causes dangerous blood pressure spikes — hypertensive crisis.
